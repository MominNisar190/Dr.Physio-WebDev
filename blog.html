🖐 Manual Therapy

Benefits of Manual Therapy

Manual therapy can effectively reduce pain and improve mobility — but not for the reasons most people think.

The Conventional View

Manual therapy is traditionally sold as a way to "realign" joints, "break up" scar tissue, or physically release tight fascia to reduce pain and improve mobility.

The Evidence-Based Reality

The mechanical narratives are largely false. You cannot physically "break up" scar tissue with your hands. Instead, manual therapy works via neurophysiological mechanisms — touch stimulates mechanoreceptors, sending novel input to the CNS, which temporarily down-regulates pain perception and decreases muscle guarding.

💡 Clinical Application: Manual therapy is a catalyst, not a cure. The temporary relief must immediately be exploited by performing active movement and strength training in the newly acquired, pain-free range of motion.

🏃 Rehabilitation

How Rehabilitation Exercises Aid Recovery

Exercise is the gold standard — but the industry suffers from a massive problem: chronic under-dosing.

The Evidence-Based Reality

Physiotherapists frequently prescribe exercises that are too light or lack progressive overload out of a misplaced fear of exacerbating symptoms. To create actual tissue adaptation, the load must exceed the tissue's current capacity.

True rehabilitation demands the principles of strength and conditioning: mechanical tension, metabolic stress, and progressive overload.

💡 Clinical Application: Rehab plans must transition from low-threshold motor control exercises (early stage) to heavy, functional loading (late stage). Pain during rehab (3–4/10) is often necessary and safe, particularly in tendinopathy management.

💊 Pain Management

Pain Management Strategies

The structural-biomedical model of pain is scientifically obsolete. Pain does not strictly equal tissue damage.

The Evidence-Based Reality

Many people have herniated discs or torn rotator cuffs with zero pain, while others have debilitating pain with completely normal MRI scans. Pain is a complex output generated by the brain, influenced by the Biopsychosocial Model — biology, psychology, and sociology.

Chronic pain represents a sensitized nervous system that is misinterpreting safe signals as dangerous ones.

💡 Clinical Application: Patients must be taught that "hurt does not equal harm." Treatment involves graded exposure — slowly reintroducing feared movements — alongside sleep optimization and stress management.

⚽ Sports

Recovering from Sports Injuries

The R.I.C.E. protocol is dead — even its creator has publicly retracted it. Here's what actually works.

The Evidence-Based Reality

Inflammation is the first essential stage of the healing cascade. Ice suppresses the release of macrophages, thereby delaying the removal of cellular debris and actually slowing tissue repair.

The modern standard is P.E.A.C.E. & L.O.V.E. — Protect, Elevate, Avoid anti-inflammatories, Compress, Educate & Load, Optimism, Vascularization, Exercise.

💡 Clinical Application: Sports rehab requires optimal loading and bridging the gap between clinical strength and the chaotic, high-velocity demands of the actual sport through plyometrics and reactive agility training.

📚 Soft-tissue injuries simply need PEACE and LOVE – BJSM

🧘 Posture

The Posture Myth — Debunked

Decades of research show almost zero correlation between spinal alignment and the presence of pain.

The Evidence-Based Reality

People with perfectly straight spines experience severe back pain, and people with severe scoliosis often have no pain at all. The problem is not the shape of the posture, but the duration. Holding any posture for 8 hours will cause ischemia and discomfort.

💡 Clinical Application: The best posture is your next posture. Treatment should focus on building strength and endurance to tolerate different positions, and promoting movement variability throughout the day.

📚 Fall of the Postural-Structural-Biomechanical Model – JBMT

🏥 Post-Surgery

Post-Surgical Rehab: What to Expect

Time-based healing is an average, not an absolute. Criterion-based rehab is the modern standard.

The Evidence-Based Reality

Returning a patient to activity simply because "six months have passed" is a primary reason re-injury rates are high. Rehabilitation must be criterion-based — a patient earns the right to progress only when they pass specific objective tests (limb symmetry index, peak torque, psychological readiness).

💡 Clinical Application: Safe recovery includes early mobilization to prevent arthrofibrosis and neuromuscular electrical stimulation (NMES) to prevent severe muscle atrophy in the days immediately following surgery.

📚 Criterion-Based Rehabilitation – JOSPT

⚡ Electrotherapy

The Truth About Electrotherapy

A large portion of electrotherapy is high-tech placebo. Here's what the evidence actually shows.

The Evidence-Based Reality

Extensive systematic reviews have found that therapeutic ultrasound is largely ineffective for almost all musculoskeletal conditions. TENS can provide acute pain relief via the Gate Control Theory, but it does not change the biological state of the tissue.

💡 Clinical Application: Electrotherapy should be used only as a temporary pain modulator to help a highly irritable patient participate in their active exercise program — never as a primary treatment.

📚 Effectiveness of TENS and Ultrasound – Cochrane Library

🧍 Ergonomics

Ergonomics: What Actually Prevents Injury

Strict ergonomic interventions have largely failed to reduce injury rates. Here's what works instead.

The Evidence-Based Reality

Disc herniations are more highly correlated with genetics, poor sleep, and abrupt spikes in physical load than with the specific mechanics of a lift. Forcing someone into a rigid "90-90-90 degree" desk setup does not prevent pain if they remain entirely sedentary.

💡 Clinical Application: Introduce micro-breaks, vary daily tasks, improve cardiovascular fitness, and manage workplace psychosocial stress. These are vastly more effective than buying a $1,000 ergonomic chair.

📚 Workplace Ergonomic Interventions – Cochrane Review

👴 Geriatric Care

Why Seniors Need to Lift Heavy

The standard approach to geriatric physiotherapy is steeped in accidental ageism. Older adults need to lift heavy.

The Evidence-Based Reality

Older adults do not lose their ability to adapt to physical stress — they simply adapt slower. Sarcopenia and osteopenia are the primary drivers of falls and loss of independence. You cannot reverse these conditions with 2-pound pink dumbbells.

Heavy resistance training is the only proven way to increase bone mineral density and stimulate fast-twitch muscle fibers — the exact fibers required to catch yourself when you trip.

💡 Clinical Application: Geriatric care should look like athletic performance training — heavy compound movements, reactive balance training, and cardiovascular conditioning.

📚 Resistance Training for Older Adults – NSCA Position Stand

🏠 Home Care

Why Home Physiotherapy Works

Home-based physiotherapy offers comfort, convenience, and personalised attention that clinic visits can't match.

The Evidence-Based Reality

Research consistently shows that patients who receive physiotherapy in their home environment demonstrate better adherence to exercise programs, faster functional recovery, and higher satisfaction scores compared to clinic-based care.

The home environment also allows the physiotherapist to assess real-world functional challenges — stairs, furniture height, bathroom setup — and tailor the rehabilitation program accordingly.

💡 Clinical Application: Home visits allow for environment-specific rehabilitation, reducing the gap between clinical performance and real-world function — the ultimate goal of physiotherapy.
